⚡ LEVEL 12: Compound Interest ⚡

TEKS 8.12(D) - Calculate and compare simple and compound interest

🎮 Guided Quest: Master the Strategy

🔧 DESMOS STRATEGY: 7-Step Process

  • Define Compound Formula: Type C ~ P(1+r)^t (use tilde ~ for ONE equation)
  • Set Principal: Type P = [amount]
  • Convert Rate to Decimal: Type r = [percent]/100 (e.g., 2% becomes 2/100)
  • Set Time: Type t = [years]
  • Simple Interest Formula: Type I = P·r·t (using · or *)
  • Simple Total: Type S = P + I
  • Find Difference: Type C - S to see how much more compound earns!
💡 KEY INSIGHT: Compound interest earns interest ON THE INTEREST! That's why C is always bigger than S. The difference might be small, but it grows over time.
Jack plans to deposit $500 into a new bank account that earns 2% interest compounded annually. He will make no more deposits or withdrawals.

Over a period of 2 years, how much MORE will he earn in interest on this account than he would if he earned simple interest at a 2% annual rate?

📝 Follow the 7 steps in Desmos:

Line 1: C ~ P(1+r)^t

Line 2: P = 500

Line 3: r = 2/100

Line 4: t = 2

Line 5: I = P·r·t

Line 6: S = P + I

Line 7: C - S

✅ The answer in line 7 is your difference!

🎉 CORRECT! You've mastered compound interest!

The Math:

  • Compound: $500(1.02)² = $520.20
  • Simple: $500 + ($500 × 0.02 × 2) = $520.00
  • Difference: $520.20 - $520.00 = $0.20

The extra $0.20 comes from earning interest on the first year's interest!
